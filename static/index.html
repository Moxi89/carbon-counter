Frequently Asked Questions

Greenhouse gases (GHGs) are atmospheric gases that trap heat in Earth's atmosphere. The main GHGs include carbon dioxide (CO‚ÇÇ), methane (CH‚ÇÑ), and nitrous oxide (N‚ÇÇO). These gases create a "greenhouse effect" by absorbing and retaining heat, leading to global warming and climate change.

Global CO‚ÇÇ emissions reached a record high of 36.8 billion tonnes in 2022, driven by increased fossil fuel use and industrial activities.

Renewable energy sources like solar, wind, and hydropower help reduce carbon emissions by providing clean alternatives to fossil fuels. They play a crucial role in transitioning to a low-carbon economy and fighting climate change.

Transportation is a major source of carbon emissions, accounting for about 20% of global CO‚ÇÇ emissions. This includes emissions from cars, trucks, planes, ships, and other vehicles that primarily run on fossil fuels.

Carbon offsets are investments in environmental projects that reduce or remove greenhouse gas emissions to compensate for emissions made elsewhere. These projects can include reforestation, renewable energy development, or methane capture.
