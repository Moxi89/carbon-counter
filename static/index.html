Frequently Asked Questions

Greenhouse gases (GHGs) are gases in Earth's atmosphere that trap heat, similar to how a greenhouse works. The main GHGs include:

  • Carbon dioxide (COâ‚‚) - from burning fossil fuels
  • Methane (CHâ‚„) - from agriculture and waste
  • Nitrous oxide (Nâ‚‚O) - from fertilizers and industrial processes

These gases create a "greenhouse effect" by trapping heat in the atmosphere, leading to global warming and climate change. COâ‚‚ is the most significant contributor due to its abundance and long atmospheric lifetime.

In 2022, global COâ‚‚ emissions reached a record high of approximately 40.6 billion tonnes (Gt). This includes:

  • Fossil fuel combustion: 36.8 Gt
  • Land use changes: 3.8 Gt

This increase occurred despite international efforts to reduce emissions, highlighting the challenge of transitioning to a low-carbon economy.

Renewable energy is crucial in reducing COâ‚‚ emissions by replacing fossil fuels. Key benefits include:

  • Zero direct COâ‚‚ emissions during operation
  • Decreasing costs making them more competitive
  • Job creation in the green energy sector

In 2022, renewables provided about 29% of global electricity generation, with rapid growth in solar and wind power.

Transportation is a major source of COâ‚‚ emissions, accounting for about 20% of global emissions. This includes:

  • Road vehicles: 72% of transport emissions
  • Aviation: 11% of transport emissions
  • Shipping: 14% of transport emissions

Electric vehicles and improved public transport are key strategies for reducing these emissions.

Carbon offsets are activities that compensate for COâ‚‚ emissions by:

  • Funding renewable energy projects
  • Supporting reforestation efforts
  • Implementing energy efficiency measures

While offsets can help, they should be combined with direct emissions reductions for maximum impact.
