Understanding Carbon Tracking

Carbon tracking is the process of measuring and monitoring carbon dioxide emissions. Here's what you need to know to understand our live counter:

What is Carbon Tracking?

Carbon tracking measures CO2 emissions from various sources including power plants, vehicles, industry, and daily activities. It makes the invisible problem of climate change visible through real numbers.

Key Insight: Without tracking, we can't understand our impact on climate change.

Why It Matters

With global emissions at 42.2 billion tons per year and only 170 billion tons remaining in the carbon budget, tracking helps us understand the urgency of climate action.

Key Insight: We have limited time to act before exceeding critical climate thresholds.

Real Impact

Every second, 1,338 tons of CO2 enter our atmosphere. This equals the annual emissions of 267,600 cars running continuously, making the scale immediately visible.

Key Insight: Individual seconds have massive cumulative impact.

The Science Behind the Numbers

The Calculation

Our live counter uses this simple but powerful formula:

Global Emissions Per Second
42.2 billion tons/year ÷ 31,536,000 seconds/year = 1,338 tons/second

This calculation gives us real-time emissions data with 2-3% accuracy.

Accuracy and Reliability

Our data is scientifically validated and updated regularly. The 2-3% margin of error accounts for variations in emissions patterns and reporting delays.
