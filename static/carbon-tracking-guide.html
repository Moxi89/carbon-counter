What Is Carbon Tracking?

Definition

Carbon tracking is the process of measuring and monitoring carbon dioxide (CO2) emissions from various sources, including industrial activities, transportation, and daily human activities.

Why It Matters

With global emissions reaching 42.2 billion tons in 2025 and only 170 billion tons remaining in the carbon budget, tracking helps us understand our impact on climate change.

Real Impact

Every second, 1,338 tons of CO2 enter our atmosphere. Carbon tracking makes this invisible problem visible and actionable.

How Carbon Tracking Works

1

Data Collection

Gather emissions data from power plants, vehicles, industries, and other sources using sensors and monitoring systems.

2

Real-time Calculation

Convert energy consumption, fuel usage, and activity data into CO2 equivalent emissions using established conversion factors.

3

Live Updates

Display current emissions rates and cumulative totals in real-time, making the scale of emissions immediately visible.

4

Trend Analysis

Track changes over time, identify patterns, and measure the impact of reduction efforts and policy changes.
