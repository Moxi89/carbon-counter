5 Simple Ways to Cut Your CO₂ at Home

5 min read January 19, 2025

In This Article

  • 3. Window Management

Want to save money AND help the planet? Good news - they go hand in hand! These simple changes can save you up to $50 per month while reducing your CO₂ emissions. No expensive gadgets needed - just smart choices that work anywhere.

1. The "Power Walk" (Takes 3 Minutes)

Try this quick walk around your home. Look for things that are plugged in but not being used. Phone chargers with no phones? TV on standby? These energy vampires are drinking up power right now.

Quick Fix:

Unplug what you're not using. Or use a power strip - one switch turns everything off.

2. The Temperature Trick

Small temperature changes make a big difference. Moving your cooling just 1°C/2°F warmer or heating 1°C/2°F cooler can cut your energy use by 7%.

Try This:

Start with a small change. You'll barely notice the difference, but your energy bill will!

3. Let's Talk About Windows

Your windows can be your best friends or your worst enemies when it comes to energy. During hot days, close blinds or curtains when the sun is strongest. When it's cold, let that sunshine in - it's free heating!

Simple Step:

Make it a habit to check your windows in the morning. Takes 30 seconds, saves hours of wasted energy.

4. The Washing Game

Did you know? Washing clothes at 30°C/86°F instead of 40°C/104°F uses about 40% less energy. And cold water cleans just as well for most loads.

Bonus Tip:

Full loads are your friend. They use the same energy as half loads but clean more clothes.

5. Light Bulb Moment

Still using old light bulbs? LED bulbs might cost a bit more upfront, but they use 90% less energy and last years longer. Start with just one bulb - maybe that light you use most.

Start Small:

Change one bulb this week. Then maybe one next month. Small steps add up!

What's Next?

Pick just one of these tips to try today. Don't worry about doing everything at once. Small changes, done regularly, make a real difference.

Remember: Every bit of energy you save helps both your wallet and our planet. Which tip will you try first?
